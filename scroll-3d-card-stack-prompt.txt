Create a high-end, scroll-driven 3D Card Stack Flip animation using Vanilla HTML, CSS, and GSAP with ScrollTrigger. The goal is to build a cinematic experience where a stack of cards enters the screen, the cover card flips away to reveal the cards beneath, and then the revealed cards fly off the screen one by one as the user scrolls.

Follow these specific instructions to recreate the effect:

### 1. Structure & Layout
- Use a single HTML file containing inline CSS and JavaScript.
- Import GSAP core and ScrollTrigger via CDN.
- Import Google Fonts: 'Outfit' (headings) and 'Inter' (body).
- **Containers**:
  - `.scroll-spacer`: A tall div (e.g., 400vh) to create scrollable space.
  - `#visual-viewport`: A fixed, 100vh, 100vw container pinned to the screen. It must have `perspective: 2000px;` to enable 3D space.
- **Elements inside the viewport**:
  - A subtle background glow div.
  - A `.hero-header` with a large title and subtitle.
  - A `.card-stack` container (`transform-style: preserve-3d;`).
  - Inside the `.card-stack`, create 5 `.card` elements: 1 Cover Card and 4 Back Cards.
  - A subtle scroll indicator at the bottom.

### 2. Design & Styling (Pastel Aesthetics)
- **Colors**: Use a soft pastel palette. 
  - Background: Soft White (`#F5F5F0`).
  - Cover Card: Dusty Rose (`#E8C4BC`).
  - Back Cards (1 to 4): Muted Sage (`#C8D8CC`), Pale Lavender (`#D4C9E3`), Soft Peach (`#F0D9D1`), Muted Blue (`#B8D4E8`).
- **Typography**: 
  - Hero text: Massive, bold ('Outfit', 900 weight), uppercase.
  - Card text: Clear numbers at the top left, large bold headings, and small descriptive text.
- **Card Styling**: 
  - Absolute positioning to stack them on top of each other.
  - Dimensions: aspect ratio roughly 3.2 / 4.2.
  - Rounded corners (e.g., 28px), subtle box-shadow, and a semi-transparent white border.
  - `backface-visibility: hidden;` is crucial.
- **3D Initial State**:
  - Use aggressive `translateZ` on the cards to prevent 3D clipping.
  - The Cover Card should face forward.
  - The 4 Back Cards must initially face backward (`transform: rotateY(180deg)`) and be positioned behind the cover card in Z-space.
  - The entire `.card-stack` should start off-screen at the bottom (`translateY(120vh) rotateX(15deg)`).

### 3. GSAP Animation Logic
Create a master GSAP timeline attached to ScrollTrigger. The trigger is `.scroll-spacer`, starting at "top top" and ending at "bottom bottom" with a `scrub` value of roughly 2.2.

**Phase 1: Entrance**
- Animate the `.hero-header` moving up and fading out (`opacity: 0`, `y: -100`).
- Simultaneously, animate the `.card-stack` into view (from its initial off-screen state to `y: 0`, `rotateX: 0`).

**Phase 2: The Flip**
- Animate the Cover Card flipping away: `rotationY: -180`, `z: -500` (pushing it far back), and `x: -120`. Use an ease like `back.inOut(1.2)`.
- At the same time, animate the 4 Back Cards flipping forward: `rotationY: 0`.
- **Crucial**: During this flip, recalculate the Z-index/Z-translation of the Back Cards so that Card 1 ends up on top (closest to the viewer) and Card 4 is at the back. Add a slight stagger to this flip.

**Phase 3: The Dismissal**
- Loop through the 4 Back Cards and animate them flying off the screen one by one.
- Each card should animate up (`y: -150vh`), scale down slightly, and tilt (`rotationX: 20`).
- Alternate their X trajectories: even cards move left (`x: -20vw`, `rotationZ: -15`), odd cards move right (`x: 20vw`, `rotationZ: 15`).
- Add appropriate delays so they leave sequentially.
- Synchronize the background glow to change color dynamically to match the color of the card currently leaving the screen.

**Phase 4: Exit**
- Fade out the entire `.card-stack` at the very end of the timeline.

### 4. Code Quality & Responsiveness
- Ensure the code is heavily commented, explaining the 3D math and GSAP logic so beginners can understand.
- Make the layout responsive: shrink the card dimensions and typography for mobile screens. Ensure the 3D transforms still look good on smaller devices.
- Do not use any premium GSAP plugins, only standard GSAP and ScrollTrigger.
